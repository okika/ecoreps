EcoReps Composting Committee

If you have food scraps and don't want to just toss them...join composting!

"The Composting Committee wants YOU to compost!"

The composting committee participates in Columbia's efforts to minimize food wastes. Currently, the Rocket processes food scraps from John Jay dining hall on a purely vegeterian diet so as to reduce odor. The Rocket has a 14-day turnover period with automated temperature and mixing controls that produces high quality compost. The committee is focusing on getting the student body to directly contribute to the composter, so check back here or keep an eye out for our training sessions so you too can start feeding The Rocket!

Meet the Composting Committee Head!

Shim Roh

Hi, my name is Shim Roh. I'm a bay area native, part of the class of 2016, and a biochem major. For part of my work experience, I worked on an organic farm and dealt a lot with composting, which sparked my interest in composting with EcoReps. Currently, my committee is using the Rocket, a $30,000 composter that Columbia purchased for our club, to recycle food waste produced by the dining halls. We also always have several projects running concurrently while we compost.

Ruggles

Ruggles Hall is the home of The Rocket and the starting point for our student-involvement initiative.

Vegetarian

We only feed The Rocket vegetable scraps - meat, bones, dairy etc that could lead to odors are avoided.

Composting Bins

Ruggles residents (or any Columbia affiliates) who sign up and attend a training session can receive a composting bin to store scraps in between trips to The Rocket.
